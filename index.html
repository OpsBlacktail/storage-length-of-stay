Portfolio-Level Findings

1. Black Label Has 2x the Turnover of Traditional Storage

Black Label's 4.6-month average stay translates to ~2.63 unit turns per year, compared to ~1.27 at Bison North and ~1.41 at Bison Central. This means significantly higher turnover costs (cleaning, re-leasing, vacancy days) but also more frequent re-pricing opportunities. If market rates are rising, Black Label captures upside faster. If rates soften, exposure is higher.

2. Mid-Size Units Are the Retention Sweet Spot

Across the portfolio, the 100-250 SF range (10x10, 10x20, 20x10) consistently delivers the longest stays: 9.6 to 11.1 months. These units likely serve tenants with stable, ongoing storage needs (business inventory, household overflow). Both the smallest units (5x10 at 6.2 months) and the largest garage condos (14x50 at 3.1 months) turn faster, suggesting more transient or project-based use cases.

3. Bison Central Drives Volume, Bison North Drives Retention

Bison Central accounts for 54% of all past rentals (213 of 393) and offers the widest unit mix. Bison North, despite having only 2 unit types and 81 rentals, achieves the highest facility average stay at 9.4 months. This suggests Bison North's larger units attract a stickier tenant base.

4. Black Label Garage Condos Behave Differently Than Traditional Storage

The garage condo product at Black Label shows a clear inverse relationship between unit size and length of stay. The largest units (14x50, 17x50) have the shortest stays (3.1 and 4.2 months), likely because tenants use them for short-term vehicle or equipment staging, not long-term storage. This is a fundamentally different demand pattern than Bison's traditional self-storage.

Operational Implications

Revenue Optimization
  • Black Label should lean into shorter commitment terms with premium pricing, given natural turnover
  • Bison facilities can justify longer-term lease incentives (6-month or 12-month discounts) since tenants naturally stay 8-10 months anyway
  • Annual rate increases at Bison capture more value per tenant given longer retention
Marketing Strategy
  • Black Label needs a larger top-of-funnel pipeline to compensate for higher turnover
  • Bison Central 5x10 and 10x30 units churn faster (6.0-6.2 months). Evaluate whether pricing or positioning adjustments could improve retention
  • 10x10 units at Bison Central are the "stickiest" product in the portfolio. Prioritize occupancy of this unit type
CapEx Considerations
  • Black Label units see 2-3x more move-in/move-out cycles per year. Budget for higher wear-and-tear on doors, flooring, and access points
  • Bison North's lower turnover means deferred maintenance can be scheduled more predictably
Data Gaps to Address
  • 12x40 at Black Label only has 4 data points. Too small for reliable conclusions
  • 10x30 at Bison Central only has 10 rentals. Same limitation applies
  • Missing: vacancy days between rentals, revenue per unit type, and move-out reason codes

Bottom Line

The Bison facilities operate like traditional self-storage with 8-10 month average retention and stable tenant bases. Black Label operates more like a short-term rental product with sub-5-month stays and high turnover. These two product types require different pricing strategies, marketing budgets, and operational workflows. Managing them with the same playbook leaves value on the table.
